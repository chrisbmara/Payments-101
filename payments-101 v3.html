Chapter 1 · The fundamentals

What actually is a payment?

Strip away the jargon and a payment is just this: value moving from someone who owes it to someone who is owed it. In our world that takes a few forms — one business paying another (B2B), a business paying consumers like payroll or refunds (B2C), a consumer paying a business at checkout (C2B), or a company simply moving its own cash between accounts. Whichever it is, the same four players are always involved:

Every client sits on two sides of that flow

Most businesses need to both get paid and pay out — and we help with each side. Keeping these two directions straight is half the battle early on.

Getting paid · Accept / Pay-in

The money coming in. When a customer checks out — by card, digital wallet, bank transfer, or bill pay — we help the business accept it. This is the world of merchant services and payment acceptance (the C2B side).

cards · digital wallets · online checkout · bill pay
Paying out · Disburse / Pay-out

The money going out, plus managing the cash in between. Paying suppliers, staff, and partners, and keeping accounts funded. This is the world of treasury services (the B2B and B2C side).

payroll · supplier payments · disbursements · liquidity
Decode the words you'll hear constantly: clearing, settlement & reconcilement
Clearing
The banks swap the instructions — who's paying whom, how much, from which account. Think of it as agreeing on the receipt.
Settlement
The actual money moves between the banks. Think of it as the cash changing hands.
Reconcilement 202-level
Matching the money that actually moved against what you expected — tying each payment back to its invoice or record so the books stay clean. It's where payments meets accounting, and it's the next layer up from the basics here.

Clearing and settlement can happen seconds apart (a real-time payment) or a couple of days apart (a standard bank transfer). That gap is exactly why we have different rails — and it's the single most useful idea to hold onto.

Our clients trust us to handle the following

🏦 Treasury services

The umbrella term for helping a company manage its money: collecting what it's owed (receivables), paying what it owes (payables), and seeing it all in one place. When someone says "treasury," picture the company's money-control room.

a.k.a. cash management

💧 Liquidity

Having the right amount of cash, in the right place, at the right time. We help clients sweep and pool cash across many accounts, entities, and countries so idle money earns its keep and no account runs dry.

keywords: sweeps · pooling · cash concentration

🔌 Digital Experience

How clients actually trigger the payments flow: online portals, mobile apps, file uploads, and increasingly APIs that connect payments straight into their own systems for real-time balances, automatic reconciliation, and reporting.

portal · file transmissions · APIs

ACH — the cheap workhorse: scheduled, batched, everywhere. Never instant.
